# StudyPilot Week 6 To Week 7 Comparison

This note compares the Week 6 and Week 7 StudyPilot milestones in instructional terms.

Comparison context:

* earlier version: milestones/week06-apis-and-app-architecture
* later version: milestones/week07-rba-project-framing
* context: week-to-week milestone progression

## 1. Short Summary Of The Overall Difference

Week 6 focuses on architecture recognition through separated file responsibilities and simulated JSON-style responses.

Week 7 shifts away from implementation structure and toward project framing.

The instructional change is from recognizing how the app is organized to explaining why the project was bounded, structured, and governed the way it was.

## 2. What Was Added

Week 7 adds these new ideas:

* explicit project intent
* visible constraints and non-goals
* explanation of structure choices across versions
* accountable AI-use framing
* a direct connection between StudyPilot and RBA-style governance

## 3. What Was Reorganized Or Improved

Week 7 improves the StudyPilot sequence by making the decision layer visible.

Instead of only showing code and structure, it explains the reasoning behind those choices.

This is a refinement of the overall project framing rather than a new implementation target.

## 4. What Is Still Intentionally Missing

Week 7 still keeps several things out of scope:

* unnecessary new features
* a hidden rebuild of StudyPilot
* abstract theory with no project connection
* extra framework expansion

Those concepts are intentionally still missing so the main lesson stays on project intent, constraints, structure choices, and governed AI use.

## 5. Why This Version Is Appropriate For Its Place In The Sequence

Week 7 is appropriate because it helps students and instructors step back from implementation and examine project reasoning.

* new concept added: RBA-style framing, constraints, and accountability language
* refinement of prior concept: Week 6 structure choices are now explained rather than only shown
* concept still intentionally absent: capstone-style packaging and final polish are still saved for Week 8

This matches the milestone sequence well.

After students have seen how StudyPilot can be implemented and structured, Week 7 explains how those decisions were made and why the project remained bounded.
