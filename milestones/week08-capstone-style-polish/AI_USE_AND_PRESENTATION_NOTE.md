# AI Use And Presentation Note

This note explains how AI use can be discussed responsibly in relation to StudyPilot.

## AI Use Position

AI was useful as a drafting, transformation, and explanation aid.

It helped accelerate milestone creation, structural re-expression, comparison notes, and documentation support.

That does not mean AI replaced project ownership.

## Human Ownership Position

Human judgment still governed:

* the purpose of the project
* the weekly milestone boundaries
* which features were allowed or excluded
* whether a preview stayed instructional rather than becoming a hidden requirement
* whether the generated artifacts matched the intended course alignment

## How To Present This Clearly

A strong presentation line is:

* AI helped draft and accelerate the work, but the project remained human-governed in scope, structure, validation, and acceptance.

That framing is more accurate than saying either:

* AI built everything on its own

or:

* AI had no meaningful role

## RBA Connection

StudyPilot is a useful RBA example because it shows:

* intent framed first
* AI operating inside governed prompts and milestone boundaries
* revision when outputs exposed drift or defects
* reality contact through execution, checks, and comparison

## Week 8 Use

In a capstone-style presentation, this note helps justify:

* why AI use was appropriate
* why boundaries mattered
* why explanation and validation are part of responsible project delivery

That is the main Week 8 AI-use lesson.
